The Problem

Three interconnected failures holding JUTC back

Riders

  • No arrival visibility at stops
  • Fare deductions with no record
  • Smart card is a black box

Operations

  • Revenue leaking through cash & overrides
  • Fleet dispatched without real-time visibility
  • No demand data for planning

The Institution

  • Eroding trust, declining ridership
  • Financial performance gap widening
  • No evidence base for capital decisions

Why Now

Three conditions aligned for the first time

01

Mobile penetration in Jamaica has crossed the threshold for a transit app to reach meaningful scale — including students and older commuters.

02

Smart card infrastructure already exists in the JUTC ecosystem. What's missing is the transparent digital layer above it.

03

Funders, regulators, and government increasingly demand evidence-based fleet investment decisions. JUTC cannot provide that without a data platform.

Competitive Positioning

Designed for JUTC's real constraints. Not a lab.

Offline-first

Works without a data connection

QR tickets, cached maps, trip logs, and fare validation run on-device. Events sync when connectivity returns.

Mixed Fleet Ready

No full hardware replacement needed

Phase 1 runs on driver smartphones only. Dedicated GPS and validator hardware layers in during Phase 2.

Cash-to-Digital Path

Riders don't need a bank account

Voucher top-ups, mobile wallet integrations, and physical top-up vendor support built in from day one.

Low-Literacy UX

3 taps, plain language

Key information visible in 3 seconds. Large touch targets for standing riders and older users. No jargon.
